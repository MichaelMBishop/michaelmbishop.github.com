Interests

I use data to answer questions. Some people call this data science. I enjoy forecasting, experimental design (A/B testing), causal inference from observational data, segmentation/cluster analysis, and data visualization. Like many geeks, I'm fond of machine learning methods, but before worrying about estimators and algorithms it's essential to ask the right questions.

My academic research consists of two streams. The first is the large-scale study of individuals and teams competing in a political forecasting tournament. What characterizes good individual judgment and good team process in this context? How can we distill knowledge from diverse sources and avoid overconfidence? I seek answers to these questions as a University of Pennsylvania based researcher for The Good Judgment Project.

My second stream of research is the study of status, norms, and academic behavior. In this project I use social network data from the Longitudinal Study of Adolescent Health to understand the relationship between family background, behavior, and popularity with one's peers.
